Mise en Place

Chef's Note

"You're not gonna get anything from just standing around."

Method

1

Preheat your mindset

Heat and oil the pan. Decide exactly what you're aiming for and lock in. Standing around yields zero results.

2

Set up your workspace

Mise en place: Ensure all tools are ready and within immediate reach. Eliminate distractions. Follow through with the plan, but be ready to adapt when it inevitably goes off the rails.

3

Train regularly

Mix the batter evenly. Work both the mind and the body. Consistency is key.

4

Pour the batter

Don't just stare at the mix. Pour it in the pan already. There is no perfect moment, take initiative.

5

Break it down

Watch closely and flip when ready. Analyze the problem, pick it apart, identify opportunities, and rebuild it in a way that benefits you.

6

Stay disciplined

Don't leave it unattended; it'll burn. Show up even when motivation decides to take a vacation.

7

Check the flavor

Taste and adjust. Review progress. There is no need to stick with a bad plan out of pure pride.

8

Lighten it up

Experiment with flavors. Joke around. You don't have to take everything so seriously.

Serve and enjoy

Finish your dish knowing you played it smart and you're eating well.
